BORRELIA MIYAMOTOI

...the other Lyme Disease

There is a more recently discovered tick-transmitted bacterial disease out there, and while its symptoms can be similar - and sometimes more severe - than that of its relative Borrelia burgdorferi (which causes Lyme Disease) this spirochete can't be diagnosed using the same test.

The organism, called Borrelia miyamotoi, was discovered in 1995 in Japan and made itself known in the United States in 2013. According to a case-series published online in the Annals of Internal Medicine, the disease is becoming more common and should be considered in all areas where deer tick-transmitted infections are endemic.

According to the report, the timing of Borrelia miyamotoi disease peak incidence suggests that, unlike the transmission of Lyme disease, the newer infections might be transmitted by unfed larval ticks.
