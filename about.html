What is Borrelia Miyamotoi?

Borrelia miyamotoi is a spirochete bacterium in the genus Borrelia. A zoonotic bacterium, B. miyamotoi can be transferred to humans through the hard (Ixodes) ticks, the same tick species that spread B. burgdorferi, the causative agent of Lyme disease, and Babesia microti, the causative agent of babesiosis.

Although the novelty and wide geographic distribution of B. miyamotoi have been recognized for several years now, this spirochete received comparatively little attention until human cases of a relapsing fever-like disease from B. miyamotoi infection were reported in 2011 in Russia, and subsequently in the United States, Europe, and Japan.
